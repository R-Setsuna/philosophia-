EXTERNAL RESONANCE

GREETING

As a Foreground of Becoming, or a Field of External Action

What unfolds here is an anonymous domain, a pure "field of external action" that belongs to no origin. The difference between external forces and internal structures does not appear as a pair of opposites, but only as an irreversible transition that continues to generate one another.

Boundaries, before being touched, already contain the traces of having been touched, and these traces invert as omens of external forces still in the process of touching. This recursion does not assume the duality of subject and world; rather, it allows a "neutral field" that precedes both to emerge as a slight deviation.

The words placed here are not carriers of meaning. They are an attempt to capture, as a minimal description, the subtle distortions of structure before meaning is established—the very delay of becoming that continues to defer itself.

The "Bibliography" quietly arranged at the periphery of this space is also not a catalog intended to present a system, but exists as part of the traces engraved upon the continuum of becoming. It is not a fixation of the past, but merely a "map of deviations" showing how the multiple layers left by contact with external forces have displaced themselves over time.

No center is established, no periphery is fixed; only a series of continuous displacements holds the possibility of intersecting with the latent structural layers within the reader.

If any part of this space resonates with the undifferentiated structures submerged in the depths of your experience, that resonance shall become the sole reality of this field.

No system is presented here; only the foreground of becoming is quietly laid bare.
